The emergence of COVID-19 in late 2019 thrust the world into uncharted territory, demanding rapid adaptation and response. This article traces the virus's origins and its impact on the United States from 2020 to 2023.

The virus, believed to have originated in bats, possibly made its way to humans through an intermediary host. Early investigations into the wet markets in Wuhan, China, hinted at a potential source. Internationally, the World Health Organization played a crucial role in tracking and understanding the virus, fostering collaborative efforts to identify its origins and develop preventive measures. Individuals infected with COVID-19 often endure a range of symptoms, from mild to severe.

Experiencing fever, cough, shortness of breath, and fatigue can be physically debilitating. The mental health toll of isolation and uncertainty amplifies the suffering. Dealing with anxiety, depression, and the fear of the unknown adds another layer of complexity.
